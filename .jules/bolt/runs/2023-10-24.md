## Options considered
### Option A (recommended)
- Replace `out.push_str(&format!(...))` with `write!(out, ...).unwrap()` and `writeln!(out, ...).unwrap()`.
- Why it fits this repo: Uses the standard library `std::fmt::Write` to append to `String` without allocating intermediate objects. Perfect for Rust repos.
- Trade-offs: Requires `.unwrap()` because `write!` on a `String` technically returns a `fmt::Result`, though it never fails unless out of memory.

### Option B
- Write a macro `push_format!(out, ...)` to encapsulate the `unwrap()`.
- Trade-offs: Requires a new macro, slightly hiding the standard standard library API. More complex than needed.

## Decision
- Option A. It's standard idiomatic Rust for performance (avoids intermediate allocations).

## Proof method
- Structural proof (work eliminated) + why it matters. By eliminating > 80 intermediate `String` allocations, memory pressure and time spent in the allocator drops significantly for formatting workflows.
